About PassRoute

PassRoute is a free study platform that helps people pass the real-world exams that open doors — a job, a license, citizenship, or a diploma.

What we do

We build clear, accurate practice tests and study guides for the exams that matter in everyday life: the commercial driver's license (CDL), the regular driver's license, the U.S. citizenship civics test, the Digital SAT, the motorcycle permit, the GED, the food handler card, and the customs broker license exam. Everything is free to use, with no account required.

Why we built it

Good test prep is often expensive or only available in English. Many of the people who need these exams most — new drivers, immigrants, career-changers, and students — are left studying from dense official handbooks alone. PassRoute exists to make high-quality preparation free and available in six languages (English, Spanish, Russian, Turkish, Hindi and Arabic), so language is never the reason someone fails a test they're otherwise ready for.

How our content is made

Our questions and guides are written from official source material — state driver handbooks, the USCIS civics materials, the MSF Motorcycle Operator Manual, the FDA Food Code, Title 19 of the Code of Federal Regulations and the HTSUS, and the published test specifications for the SAT and GED. Every question has an explanation so you learn the reasoning, not just the answer. We review content for accuracy, but rules change and vary by location, so we always encourage you to confirm specifics with the official source for your state or situation.

Independence

PassRoute is an independent study aid. We are not affiliated with, authorized by, or endorsed by any government agency or testing organization, including the DMV, USCIS, FMCSA, CBP, the College Board or the GED Testing Service. Official trademarks belong to their respective owners and are used only to describe the exams we help you study for.

Free, and how we keep it that way

PassRoute is free because it is supported by advertising. We keep ads unobtrusive and never put them in the way of a question or an answer. If you find PassRoute useful, simply using it and sharing it with someone who's studying is the best way to support us.
