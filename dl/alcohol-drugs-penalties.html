Sample questions

Marcus, age 34, is pulled over leaving a friend's barbecue in Houston. The officer suspects he has been drinking and asks for a breath test. At what BAC level is Marcus, as an adult driver, legally considered to be Driving While Intoxicated (DWI) in Texas?

  1. 0.08% BAC or higher ✓ Correct answer
  2. 0.04% BAC or higher
  3. 0.15% BAC or higher
  4. Any detectable amount above 0.00% BAC

Why: For an adult driver age 21 or older, the Texas DWI limit is 0.08% BAC. 0.04% is the limit for commercial drivers, and any detectable amount (above 0.00%) applies only to drivers under 21 under Zero Tolerance.

Marcus, age 26, is pulled over in Austin after weaving between lanes. He passes a breath test reading 0.08% BAC and tells the officer he feels completely fine and was driving carefully. Under Texas law, what offense has Marcus committed based on that reading?

  1. Nothing, because 0.08% is the legal limit and he is only over it once the reading exceeds 0.08%
  2. Driving While Intoxicated (DWI), because driving at or above 0.08% BAC is DWI for an adult ✓ Correct answer
  3. Driving Under the Influence (DUI), because any detectable alcohol makes an adult guilty of DUI
  4. Nothing, because he told the officer he felt fine and showed no real impairment

Why: For an adult 21 or older, driving at or above 0.08% BAC is the offense of Driving While Intoxicated (DWI), so reaching exactly 0.08% counts. The Zero Tolerance DUI for any detectable alcohol applies only to drivers under 21, and feeling 'fine' does not change the legal BAC limit.

A 30-year-old driver is pulled over after weaving across lanes. A breath test reads 0.06% BAC, but the officer observes slurred speech and poor coordination consistent with alcohol impairment. Under Texas law, what can happen?

  1. Nothing, because 0.06% is below the 0.08% adult limit, so DWI cannot apply
  2. The driver is automatically cleared once the breath test reads under 0.08%
  3. The driver can still be charged with DWI because they are impaired by alcohol, even though BAC is below 0.08% ✓ Correct answer
  4. The driver can only be cited for an open-container violation, not DWI

Why: In Texas, a driver can be charged with DWI below 0.08% BAC if alcohol or drugs impair them, so the observed impairment supports a DWI charge. The 0.08% figure is a per se limit, not a safe harbor, so being under it does not automatically clear the driver.

Marcus drives a commercial tractor-trailer for a freight company. After a long shift, he has two beers with dinner and a breath test later reads 0.05% BAC. He argues he is well under the legal limit and is fine to keep driving his rig. Is he correct, and what is the relevant limit?

  1. He is correct; the limit for all drivers in Texas is 0.08% BAC, so 0.05% is legal.
  2. He is correct because any BAC under 0.08% can never result in a charge.
  3. He is over the limit because Texas has zero tolerance and any detectable alcohol is illegal for him.
  4. He is over the limit; commercial drivers have a lower limit of 0.04% BAC, and 0.05% exceeds it. ✓ Correct answer

Why: Commercial drivers in Texas are held to a lower limit of 0.04% BAC, so Marcus's 0.05% reading puts him over the limit. The 0.08% figure (A) is the standard adult limit, not the commercial one, and zero tolerance (C) applies only to drivers under 21, not commercial drivers.

Marcus is 19 and drives home from a friend's party where he had one beer. A police officer lawfully stops him and a breath test shows a BAC of 0.03%. Under Texas law, what is his legal status?

  1. He is illegal to drive — under Texas Zero Tolerance, any detectable alcohol (a BAC above 0.00) for a driver under 21 is a DUI by a minor. ✓ Correct answer
  2. He is legal to drive because his 0.03% BAC is below the 0.08% DWI limit.
  3. He is legal to drive because his 0.03% BAC is below the 0.04% commercial-driver limit.
  4. He is legal to drive because he had only one drink and the liver processes one standard drink per hour.

Why: For drivers under 21, Texas has Zero Tolerance, so ANY detectable alcohol (a BAC above 0.00) is illegal and is a DUI by a minor. The 0.08% adult DWI limit and the 0.04% commercial limit do not apply to a 19-year-old minor, making those traps wrong.

A 19-year-old leaves a party and is pulled over in Texas. A breath test shows a BAC of 0.03%, well below the adult limit of 0.08%. What offense, if any, has the driver committed?

  1. Nothing, because the BAC is under the 0.08% adult limit
  2. DUI (Driving Under the Influence) of alcohol by a minor, because any detectable alcohol is illegal under 21 ✓ Correct answer
  3. DWI, because the driver is automatically intoxicated at any BAC
  4. Nothing, but the driver's license is suspended 180 days for implied consent

Why: Texas has a Zero Tolerance law for drivers under 21: any detectable alcohol (a BAC above 0.00) is a DUI of alcohol by a minor, regardless of the 0.08% adult DWI limit. Option A wrongly applies the adult threshold to a minor, and the 180-day suspension in D is the penalty for refusing a test, not for a 0.03% reading.
