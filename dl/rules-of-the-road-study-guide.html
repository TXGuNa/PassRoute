Under Texas's basic speed law, how fast may you drive?

  1. Never faster than is safe for current conditions ✓ Correct answer
  2. Always exactly the posted speed limit
  3. As fast as your vehicle allows

Why: Texas's basic speed law means you must never drive faster than is safe for the current conditions.

In Texas, what is the default speed limit in an urban district where no sign is posted?

  1. 20 mph
  2. 30 mph ✓ Correct answer
  3. 45 mph
  4. 55 mph

Why: Where unposted, the default speed limit in a Texas urban district is 30 mph.

On a numbered highway in the daytime with no posted sign, what is the default speed limit?

  1. 30 mph
  2. 55 mph
  3. 70 mph ✓ Correct answer
  4. 85 mph

Why: Where unposted, the default daytime speed limit on numbered highways is 70 mph.

  1. 65 mph
  2. 70 mph

  4. 85 mph ✓ Correct answer

Why: Some specific Texas tollways are posted with speed limits as high as 85 mph.

What must you do in a school zone when the lights flash or children are present?

  1. Obey the posted reduced speed limit ✓ Correct answer
  2. Drive at the normal speed limit
  3. Speed up to clear the area
  4. Stop completely and wait

Why: In a school zone you must obey the posted reduced limit (often 20 mph) when lights flash or children are present.

  1. Yes, always
  2. No, it is illegal ✓ Correct answer
