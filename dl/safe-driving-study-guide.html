Sample questions

What is the three-second rule used for?

  1. Keeping a safe following distance ✓ Correct answer
  2. Knowing when to change your oil
  3. Setting your parking brake
  4. Timing how long to signal

Why: The three-second rule helps you keep a safe following distance behind the car ahead.

How do you use the three-second rule?

  1. Count three car lengths ahead
  2. Count the seconds to reach a fixed point ✓ Correct answer
  3. Drive three miles per hour slower
  4. Wait three seconds at every stop

Why: Pick a fixed point ahead and count the seconds it takes to reach it after the car ahead passes it.

When should you add more seconds to your following distance?

  1. On dry, clear roads
  2. When traffic is light
  3. In rain, fog, ice, or at high speed ✓ Correct answer
  4. When driving slowly

Why: Add more seconds to your following distance in rain, fog, ice, or at high speed.

When should you add extra seconds to the three-second following distance?

  1. Only on sunny days
  2. When the road is dry and empty
  3. When traffic is light
  4. In rain, fog, ice, or at high speed ✓ Correct answer

Why: Rain, fog, ice, and high speed all require adding extra seconds to your following distance.

When should you increase your following distance?

  1. When driving behind a motorcycle or large truck ✓ Correct answer
  2. When the road is dry and clear
  3. When traffic is light
  4. When you are in a hurry

Why: Increase your following distance behind motorcycles, large trucks, or when being tailgated.

When another driver is tailgating you, what should you do?

  1. Brake suddenly to warn them
  2. Increase your following distance ahead ✓ Correct answer
  3. Speed up to escape them
  4. Stop in the middle of the road

Why: Increasing your following distance gives you more room to stop safely when being tailgated.
