Sample questions

Driving at dusk, you approach an intersection where the sign facing you is badly faded and you can no longer read any of its lettering. You can still clearly see that the sign has eight sides. What must you do at this intersection?

  1. Come to a complete stop, because an eight-sided (octagonal) sign always means STOP. ✓ Correct answer
  2. Slow down and yield the right of way, stopping only if other traffic is present.
  3. Slow down and proceed with caution, since the shape only warns of a hazard ahead.
  4. Treat it as a school crossing and be ready for children entering the roadway.

Why: The octagon is the only eight-sided sign and always means STOP, so the shape alone tells you to come to a full stop even when the lettering is unreadable. Yielding is signaled by an inverted triangle, not an octagon, which makes that option the trap.

You are merging onto a highway from an on-ramp and see a red-and-white sign shaped like an upside-down (inverted) triangle facing you. What does this sign require you to do?

  1. Come to a complete stop and remain stopped until the highway is empty in both directions
  2. Give the right of way to traffic on the highway, slowing or stopping only if necessary to do so safely ✓ Correct answer
  3. Proceed at the posted maximum speed because you have the right of way when merging
  4. Treat it as a warning of a hazard ahead and continue with caution without yielding

Why: An inverted triangle is a YIELD sign: you must give the right of way to traffic already on the highway and slow or stop only if needed to merge safely. A full stop every time (A) describes an octagonal STOP sign, and a diamond-shaped warning sign (D) does not require yielding the right of way.

You're driving on a two-lane rural highway and want to pass a slow tractor ahead. Just before you pull out, you notice a sign shaped like a triangle lying on its side (a horizontal point) posted on the LEFT shoulder of the road. What is this sign telling you?

  1. Yield to oncoming traffic before merging back into your lane
  2. A school zone or school crossing is ahead, so reduce speed
  3. You are entering a No Passing Zone and must not pass the tractor here ✓ Correct answer
  4. A hazard or condition is ahead, so slow down and proceed with caution

Why: A pennant (a horizontal triangle) posted on the LEFT side of the road marks a No Passing Zone, so you must not pass the tractor. A yield is an inverted (upside-down) triangle, and a diamond is the warning shape, so those options confuse other sign shapes with the pennant.

You're driving on an unfamiliar two-lane highway at dusk. Up ahead on the right shoulder you see a yellow, diamond-shaped sign, but you're still too far away to read the words or symbol printed on it. Based on its shape alone, what should you immediately understand?

  1. It is a regulatory sign telling you a law you must obey, such as a speed limit
  2. It marks a school zone or school crossing ahead
  3. It is a guide sign giving directions or distances to the next town
  4. It is a warning sign alerting you to a hazard or change in road conditions ahead ✓ Correct answer

Why: A diamond shape always means a warning sign, telling you a hazard or condition lies ahead. Regulatory laws use vertical rectangles (A) and guide/direction information uses horizontal rectangles (C), while school zones use a pentagon (B).

While driving through an unfamiliar residential area, you see a yellow-green, five-sided sign with the point facing up. Based on its shape alone, what is this sign warning you about?

  1. You are approaching a school zone or school crossing ✓ Correct answer
  2. There is a railroad crossing ahead
  3. You are entering a no-passing zone
  4. A general hazard or condition lies ahead

Why: A pentagon (5-sided, point up) shape always means a school zone or school crossing. A round sign warns of a railroad crossing ahead, so option B confuses two different sign shapes.

You are driving on a rural two-lane highway and notice a round, yellow sign on the right shoulder with a black 'X' and the letters 'RR' on it, posted well before any tracks are visible. What is this sign telling you?

  1. You have reached the tracks themselves and must stop your vehicle on them
  2. A railroad crossing is ahead, so be prepared to slow down and look for trains ✓ Correct answer
  3. You are entering a school zone and must reduce your speed for children
  4. This is a no-passing zone and you may not pass other vehicles here

Why: A round (circular) sign is the advance warning that a railroad crossing lies ahead, so you should slow down and watch for trains. The crossing itself is marked by the X-shaped Crossbuck (not this round sign), and the pentagon shape, not the circle, signals a school zone.
