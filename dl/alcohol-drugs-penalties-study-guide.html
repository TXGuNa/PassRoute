Sample questions

In Texas, what is the BAC limit for DWI for an adult driver age 21 or older?

  1. 0.08% ✓ Correct answer
  2. 0.04%
  3. 0.10%
  4. 0.05%

Why: In Texas, an adult age 21 or older is legally intoxicated (DWI) at a BAC of 0.08% or higher.

In Texas, what offense is driving at or above 0.08% BAC?

  1. Speeding
  2. Driving While Intoxicated (DWI) ✓ Correct answer
  3. Reckless driving
  4. Open container violation

Why: Driving at or above 0.08% BAC is the offense called Driving While Intoxicated (DWI).

Can a driver be charged with DWI if their BAC is below 0.08%?

  1. No, never below 0.08%
  2. Only commercial drivers
  3. Yes, if impaired by alcohol or drugs ✓ Correct answer
  4. Only if they refuse a test

Why: A driver can still be charged with DWI below 0.08% BAC if impaired by alcohol or drugs.

What is the BAC limit for a commercial driver in Texas?

  1. 0.08%
  2. 0.10%
  3. 0.02%
  4. 0.04% ✓ Correct answer

Why: A commercial driver's BAC limit is 0.04%, lower than the standard adult limit of 0.08%.

In Texas, what is the legal blood alcohol limit for a driver under 21?

  1. Any amount above 0.00 is illegal ✓ Correct answer
  2. 0.08 BAC
  3. 0.04 BAC
  4. 0.05 BAC

Why: Texas uses Zero Tolerance, so any detectable alcohol (a BAC above 0.00) is illegal for a driver under 21.

What does a minor commit by driving with any detectable alcohol?

  1. Nothing illegal
  2. DUI of alcohol by a minor ✓ Correct answer
  3. A parking violation
  4. A commercial DWI

Why: Under Texas Zero Tolerance, any detectable alcohol means a minor commits DUI of alcohol by a minor.
