Sample questions

You arrive at an intersection and the traffic signal facing you is a steady green light. You want to turn left across oncoming traffic, but several cars are coming toward you through the intersection. What are you legally allowed to do?

  1. Proceed straight through or make your turn, but first yield to the oncoming cars and any traffic already in the intersection. ✓ Correct answer
  2. Turn left immediately, because a steady green light gives you a protected turn and oncoming traffic must stop for you.
  3. Stop completely behind the line and wait, treating the green like a flashing red until the oncoming cars clear.
  4. Slow down and proceed with caution without yielding, since a steady green works the same as a flashing yellow.

Why: A steady green means go only when the intersection is clear; you may proceed straight or turn, but you must yield to oncoming traffic and vehicles already in the intersection. Option B is the trap: only a green arrow gives a protected turn where oncoming traffic is stopped, not a steady green light.

You are driving toward an intersection at the speed limit when the green light turns to a steady yellow. You are still about half a block away and the road behind you is clear. What should you do?

  1. Speed up to get through the intersection before the light turns red
  2. Stop before the intersection if you can do so safely, because the light is about to turn red ✓ Correct answer
  3. Treat it like a flashing yellow and continue through carefully without stopping
  4. Come to a full stop and then proceed when the way is clear, as you would for a flashing red

Why: A steady yellow light warns that the signal is about to turn red, so you should stop if you can do so safely and never speed up to beat the light. Option C describes a flashing yellow (caution) and Option D describes a flashing red (stop sign), which are different signals.

You are approaching an intersection and the traffic light facing you turns to a steady red while you are still a short distance from the marked stop line. There is no sign prohibiting a turn, and you intend to go straight ahead. What must you do?

  1. Slow down and proceed carefully through the intersection without stopping, since the light may change soon
  2. Stop only if cross traffic is present, then continue straight through the red light
  3. Come to a complete stop behind the stop line and remain stopped until the light turns green ✓ Correct answer
  4. Treat the signal as a stop sign, stop fully, then proceed straight when the way is clear

Why: A steady red light requires you to stop completely behind the stop line and remain stopped until the light turns green. Option D describes a flashing red light (treated like a stop sign), not a steady red, so proceeding straight on a steady red is wrong.

You are in the right lane approaching an intersection with a steady red light. You want to turn right. There is no sign prohibiting the turn, and cross traffic has a gap. What should you do?

  1. Slow down and turn right without stopping, since the cross traffic has a gap
  2. Treat it as a stop sign, then turn right only when a green arrow appears
  3. Wait for the light to turn green before turning right, because you may never turn on red
  4. Come to a full stop behind the line, then turn right since it is clear and no sign prohibits it ✓ Correct answer

Why: At a steady red light you must stop completely behind the line, and a right turn is permitted only after that full stop when the way is clear and no sign prohibits it. Option A skips the required full stop, and option C wrongly claims right-on-red is never allowed.

You approach an intersection late at night and the overhead traffic signal is flashing red in every direction. There is no other traffic in sight. What must you do?

  1. Come to a complete stop, then proceed through the intersection when it is safe. ✓ Correct answer
  2. Slow down, look both ways, and roll through without stopping since the way is clear.
  3. Stop and remain stopped until the flashing red changes to a steady green light.
  4. Treat it as a yield sign and merge through only if another vehicle is approaching.

Why: A flashing red light means the same thing as a stop sign: you must come to a full stop and then proceed when it is safe. Option B is wrong because a flashing red always requires a complete stop (rolling through is the rule for a flashing yellow), and option C is wrong because a flashing red signal will not turn green.

Late at night, you approach an intersection where the traffic signal is no longer running its normal cycle and is instead showing a steadily flashing YELLOW light over your lane. What should you do?

  1. Come to a complete stop behind the line, then proceed when the way is clear, as you would at a stop sign.
  2. Slow down and proceed carefully through the intersection without stopping, watching for cross traffic. ✓ Correct answer
  3. Treat the intersection as an all-way stop because the signal is not cycling normally.
  4. Stop and remain stopped until the signal returns to a steady green light.

Why: A flashing YELLOW light means caution, so you slow down and proceed carefully through the intersection without coming to a stop. A flashing RED light is the one that means a full stop like a stop sign, and a completely dark or non-working signal is the one treated as an all-way stop, so those are the wrong responses here.
