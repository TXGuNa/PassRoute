Sample questions

How far does the danger zone extend around the bus?

  1. 10 feet ✓ Correct answer
  2. 5 feet
  3. 15 feet
  4. 50 feet

Why: The danger zone is the area all around the bus, extending about 10 feet, where children are most at risk.

How far does the danger zone extend from the front, sides, and rear of the bus?

  1. 5 feet
  2. 10 feet ✓ Correct answer
  3. 15 feet
  4. 30 feet

Why: The danger zone extends about 10 feet from the front, sides, and rear of the bus.

How far in front of the bus can the danger zone reach?

  1. Up to 10 feet
  2. Up to 20 feet
  3. Up to 30 feet ✓ Correct answer
  4. Up to 50 feet

Why: The danger zone can reach up to 30 feet in front of the bus.

Which areas of the danger zone are the most dangerous?

  1. The left side and front
  2. The rear and left side
  3. Behind the bus only
  4. The front and right side/rear ✓ Correct answer

Why: The most dangerous areas are immediately in front of the bus and the right side/rear.

Which mirrors help you see the front and side danger zones?

  1. Flat, convex, and crossover ✓ Correct answer
  2. Flat and convex only
  3. Rearview and side
  4. Convex and rearview

Why: Use the outside flat, convex, and crossover mirrors to watch the front and side danger zones.

When should you adjust the school bus mirrors?

  1. Once a week
  2. Before each trip ✓ Correct answer
  3. After each trip
  4. Once a month

Why: Adjust the outside and crossover mirrors before each trip to keep every student in view.
