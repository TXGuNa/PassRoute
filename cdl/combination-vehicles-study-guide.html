Sample questions

What causes most combination-vehicle rollovers?

  1. Going too fast in a turn ✓ Correct answer
  2. Braking too hard
  3. Following too closely
  4. Locking the trailer wheels

Why: Most combination-vehicle rollovers happen from going too fast in a turn.

To prevent rollovers, how should cargo be loaded?

  1. As high as possible
  2. Low and centered ✓ Correct answer
  3. Toward the rear
  4. Toward one side

Why: Keep cargo low to maintain a low center of gravity and help prevent rollovers.

To help prevent rollovers, how should you position the load?

  1. Stacked up high
  2. Toward the rear
  3. Centered side-to-side ✓ Correct answer
  4. Shifted to one side

Why: To prevent rollovers, keep the load centered side-to-side and the cargo low.

To prevent rollovers, what should you do before curves and ramps?

  1. Speed up
  2. Release the brakes
  3. Steer sharply
  4. Slow down ✓ Correct answer

Why: To prevent rollovers, slow down before curves and ramps.

What share of truck-driver deaths in crashes are rollovers?

  1. Half ✓ Correct answer
  2. One-third
  3. One-quarter
  4. Two-thirds

Why: Half of all truck-driver deaths in crashes are rollovers.

In a turn, which wheels follow a tighter path due to off-tracking?

  1. Tractor front wheels
  2. Trailer rear wheels ✓ Correct answer
  3. Tractor drive wheels
  4. Trailer front wheels

Why: Off-tracking means the trailer's rear wheels follow a tighter inner path than the tractor's front wheels.
