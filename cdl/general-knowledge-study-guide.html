Sample questions

Total stopping distance is the sum of braking distance, reaction distance, and which other distance?

  1. Perception distance ✓ Correct answer
  2. Following distance
  3. Sight distance
  4. Tread distance

Why: Total stopping distance is perception distance plus reaction distance plus braking distance.

At 55 mph in good conditions, what is the total stopping distance?

  1. About 250 ft
  2. About 419 ft ✓ Correct answer
  3. About 350 ft
  4. About 500 ft

Why: At 55 mph in good conditions, total stopping distance is about 419 ft, roughly the length of a football field.

At 55 mph, about how far is the reaction distance?

  1. 30 feet
  2. 45 feet
  3. 60 feet ✓ Correct answer
  4. 100 feet

Why: At 55 mph, reaction distance is about 60 feet, roughly three-quarters of a second.

If you double your speed, your braking distance roughly increases by how much?

  1. Stays the same
  2. Doubles
  3. Triples
  4. Quadruples ✓ Correct answer

Why: Braking distance grows with the square of speed, so doubling your speed roughly quadruples it.

Compared to a loaded truck, an empty truck can have what kind of stopping distance?

  1. Longer ✓ Correct answer
  2. Shorter
  3. The same
  4. Always zero

Why: An empty truck can have a longer stopping distance than a loaded one because it has less traction.

Under 40 mph, how many seconds of following distance should you allow for every 10 ft of vehicle length?

  1. 0.5 seconds
  2. 1 second ✓ Correct answer
  3. 2 seconds
  4. 3 seconds

Why: Allow 1 second of following distance for every 10 ft of vehicle length when under 40 mph.
