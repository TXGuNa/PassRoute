Sample questions

During my pre-trip inspection, an examiner asks me to explain the air brake setup on my tractor. I tell him the air brakes are really several braking systems combined into one. Which answer correctly states how many separate braking systems an air brake system actually combines, and names them?

  1. Three systems: the service brakes, the parking brakes, and the emergency brakes. ✓ Correct answer
  2. Two systems: the primary air system and the secondary air system with one set of controls.
  3. Four systems: the service brakes, the parking brakes, the emergency brakes, and the ABS.
  4. One system: a single circuit that handles normal stops, parking, and emergencies together.

Why: An air brake system is actually three separate systems combined: the service brakes, the parking brakes, and the emergency brakes.

I'm doing my pre-trip inspection and I rev the engine to build air pressure while watching the dash gauge. I want to confirm the governor is working correctly by noting the pressure at which the air compressor stops pumping air into the tanks. At approximately what reading should the needle stop climbing because the governor has cut OUT?

  1. About 100 psi, which is the pressure where the compressor stops adding air to the tanks
  2. About 125 psi, at which point the governor cuts out and the compressor stops pumping ✓ Correct answer
  3. About 150 psi, where the system reaches its maximum and the compressor shuts off
  4. About 60 psi, the point at which the governor halts the compressor on a charged system

Why: The governor cuts out at about 125 psi, stopping the compressor from pumping more air into the tanks.

I'm parked doing my pre-trip and watching the air gauge. The compressor had cut out earlier and stopped pumping, so the pressure has been slowly dropping as I test the system. At what gauge reading should I expect the governor to kick the compressor back in and start pumping air again?

  1. About 60 psi, the same point where the low air pressure warning light and buzzer come on
  2. About 125 psi, the level at which the governor stops the compressor from pumping
  3. About 100 psi, the point where the governor signals the compressor to resume pumping ✓ Correct answer
  4. About 150 psi, the level at which the safety relief valve opens to release pressure

Why: The governor cut-in point is about 100 psi, where it signals the compressor to resume pumping air.

During my pre-trip, I notice the air compressor governor isn't cutting out — the gauge keeps climbing past the normal 125 psi point where pumping should stop. If the governor never cuts out, at roughly what pressure should the safety relief valve open to protect the system from over-pressurizing?

  1. At about 100 psi, the same point where the governor would normally cut in and resume pumping
  2. At about 60 psi, the same threshold that triggers the low air pressure warning light and buzzer
  3. At about 125 psi, matching the governor cut-out so the two devices act together
  4. At about 150 psi, venting excess air to keep the system from building dangerous pressure ✓ Correct answer

Why: The safety relief valve opens at about 150 psi to vent excess air and protect the system from dangerous pressure.

I'm doing a pre-trip inspection on a single tractor with a fully charged air system. With the engine off, I fan the brakes down step by step and watch the dash. At what point should the low-air warning light and buzzer be active by — that is, the warning must already be ON before pressure falls below which level?

  1. Before air pressure drops below 60 psi ✓ Correct answer
  2. Before air pressure drops below 80 psi
  3. Once pressure has already fallen into the 20 to 45 psi range
  4. Before air pressure drops below 100 psi

Why: The low air pressure warning must come on before system pressure drops below 60 psi.

I'm driving a loaded single tractor-trailer when a line ruptures and my air pressure starts bleeding off fast. The low-air warning light and buzzer are already on, and the needle keeps falling. At roughly what pressure should I expect the spring (emergency) brakes to come on by themselves and bring me to a stop?

  1. Around 60 psi, the same point where the low-air warning first activates
  2. Somewhere in the 20 to 45 psi range, after the warning has already been on ✓ Correct answer
  3. Around 100 psi, the governor cut-in point where the compressor restarts
  4. Around 5 to 10 psi, only once the tanks are almost completely empty

Why: The spring (emergency/parking) brakes apply automatically when system air pressure falls into the 20 to 45 psi range.
