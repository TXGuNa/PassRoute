Sample questions

During your pre-trip inspection, you measure tread depth and find one of your steering-axle (front) tires has worn down to 3/32 inch, while your drive and trailer tires are all at 3/32 inch as well. Which statement correctly identifies whether your vehicle is legal to drive?

  1. The vehicle is NOT legal to drive, because steering-axle tires must have at least 4/32 inch of tread. ✓ Correct answer
  2. The vehicle is legal to drive, because all tires meet the 2/32 inch minimum for every tire.
  3. The vehicle is legal to drive, because the front tires only need 2/32 inch and the rest are fine.
  4. The vehicle is NOT legal to drive, because all tires on every axle must have at least 4/32 inch of tread.

Why: Steering-axle tires must have at least 4/32 inch of tread, so a front tire at 3/32 inch is below the minimum and makes the vehicle illegal to drive.

During your pre-trip inspection you run a tread-depth gauge across all your tires. The steering-axle tires measure a healthy 5/32 inch, but one of your rear drive tires is worn down to a noticeably thinner reading. What is the LEAST tread depth that rear (non-steering) tire can legally have and still pass inspection?

  1. 1/32 inch
  2. 2/32 inch ✓ Correct answer
  3. 4/32 inch
  4. 6/32 inch

Why: All tires except the front steering axle must have at least 2/32 inch of tread depth measured in a major groove.

During your pre-trip inspection you grip the steering wheel and rock it back and forth before starting the engine. On your 20-inch diameter wheel, you can turn the rim a couple of inches in each direction before the front wheels begin to respond. At what point should this free play make you suspect a steering-system problem and pull the vehicle from service?

  1. When the wheel can be turned more than about 10 degrees (roughly 2 inches on a 20-inch wheel) before the front wheels move ✓ Correct answer
  2. Only when the wheel can be turned more than about 20 degrees before the front wheels move
  3. When the wheel can be turned more than about 5 degrees, since any detectable play is unsafe
  4. Only when free play exceeds about 45 degrees, since that is when steering becomes truly hard to control

Why: More than about 10 degrees of steering-wheel free play (roughly 2 inches on a 20-inch wheel) before the front wheels move is too much and signals a steering problem.

Your tractor-trailer has stalled on the shoulder of a two-lane undivided highway and won't restart. You switch on your hazard flashers and reach for your emergency warning kit so you can set out reflective triangles at 100 ft behind, 100 ft ahead, and 10 ft to the rear of the vehicle. To be legally equipped to place warning devices in EVERY required position like this, what is the MINIMUM number of reflective triangles your truck must carry?

  1. Two triangles, since one is placed ahead and one behind the vehicle
  2. Three triangles ✓ Correct answer
  3. One triangle placed 10 ft to the rear, plus your hazard flashers
  4. Six triangles, to be sure you can cover both directions of traffic

Why: A commercial vehicle must carry at least three reflective warning triangles as emergency warning devices.

It is the middle of your shift and a fitting on your diesel line has been weeping fuel near the engine wiring. Suddenly a small fire erupts there, fed by the leaking diesel and crackling around the live electrical harness. You reach for the extinguisher mounted in your cab. Which rating on that extinguisher tells you it is designed to fight BOTH this burning fuel and the energized electrical fire?

  1. A B:C extinguisher, rated for burning liquids and electrical fires ✓ Correct answer
  2. A Class A extinguisher, since it is the standard rating carried on commercial vehicles
  3. A Water-type extinguisher, to cool the diesel and knock the fire down fast
  4. It does not matter which rating you grab, as long as you discharge it at the base of the flames

Why: A burning flammable liquid is a Class B fire and energized electrical equipment is Class C, so a B:C-rated extinguisher is required for both.

It is 5:00 a.m. and you are finishing the pre-trip inspection on a tractor-trailer you were just assigned. Your dispatcher is rushing you to leave, but you remember that federal rules require you to carry certain emergency equipment before any trip. You did NOT find a kit of spare electrical fuses anywhere in the cab. Under what condition is it legal for you to drive away WITHOUT carrying spare fuses?

  1. Only if the truck is equipped with circuit breakers instead of fuses ✓ Correct answer
  2. Only if you are carrying a fully charged spare battery in the cab
  3. Only if the trailer is fitted with LED clearance and marker lights
  4. Only if the tractor has a backup (second) alternator for the electrical system

Why: Spare fuses aren't required when the electrical system is protected by circuit breakers, since breakers reset instead of needing replacement.
