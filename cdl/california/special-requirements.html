Before I roll, I need to decide whether I can legally drive on the highway without first obtaining a Caltrans transportation permit. Based on California's maximum width limit, what should I do?

  1. Proceed without a permit, because 102 inches (8 ft 6 in) is exactly the maximum legal width allowed in California without a permit. ✓ Correct answer
  2. Stop and get a Caltrans permit, because any load wider than 96 inches requires an oversize permit on California highways.
  3. Proceed without a permit, because the maximum legal width in California is actually 108 inches, so I have several inches to spare.
  4. Stop and get a Caltrans permit, because the legal width limit in California is only 100 inches and my load exceeds it.

Why: In California, the maximum legal width for a vehicle or load without a special permit is 102 inches (8 ft 6 in).

  2. It is extralegal; anything wider than 102 inches (8 ft 6 in) needs a Caltrans permit, so at 104 inches I must get one. ✓ Correct answer
  3. It is still legal; the limit is 108 inches, so at 104 inches I am under the threshold and need no permit.
  4. It is extralegal only above 114 inches; at 104 inches I am fine to drive permit-free.

Why: In California, the maximum legal width for a vehicle or load without a permit is 102 inches (8 ft 6 in). Any vehicle or load exceeding 102 inches is 'extralegal' and requires a Caltrans transportation permit.

  1. Nothing changes — 102 inches is the statewide maximum and applies equally on every road, including local roads, so no permit or check is ever required

  3. I must check whether the city or county has restricted the width to 96 inches on that local road, because a local agency may impose a tighter width limit than the 102-inch state maximum ✓ Correct answer

Why: In California the maximum vehicle width without a permit is 102 inches (8 ft 6 in). However, a city or county is authorized to restrict width to 96 inches on roads under its local jurisdiction.

  1. 102 inches, because California's maximum legal width is 8 feet 6 inches, which equals 102 inches. ✓ Correct answer

Why: California's maximum legal vehicle width without a permit is 102 inches. Converting to feet and inches: 102 inches divided by 12 equals 8 feet with 6 inches remaining, so 102 inches = 8 ft 6 in.
