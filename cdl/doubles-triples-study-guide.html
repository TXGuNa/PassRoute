Sample questions

Which trailer should be placed first, right behind the tractor?

  1. The most heavily loaded ✓ Correct answer
  2. The lightest loaded
  3. The empty one
  4. The shortest one

Why: Put the most heavily loaded trailer first, right behind the tractor, with lighter trailers toward the rear.

Compared to pulling one trailer, pulling two or three trailers is:

  1. More stable
  2. Less stable ✓ Correct answer
  3. Equally stable
  4. Easier to back up

Why: Pulling two or three trailers is less stable than one and increases the risk of rollover and jackknife.

In rearward amplification ("crack-the-whip"), which trailer swings the widest?

  1. The tractor
  2. The front trailer
  3. The rear trailer ✓ Correct answer
  4. The converter dolly

Why: In rearward amplification, a quick steering move makes the rear trailer swing much wider and it can roll over.

On which combination is the rearward amplification (crack-the-whip) effect worse?

  1. Singles
  2. All equally
  3. Doubles
  4. Triples ✓ Correct answer

Why: Rearward amplification grows with each added trailer, so it is worse on triples than on doubles.

To avoid rearward amplification, how should you steer?

  1. Gently and gradually ✓ Correct answer
  2. Quickly and sharply
  3. Hard into turns
  4. With sudden moves

Why: To avoid rearward amplification, steer gently and gradually, avoid quick lane changes, and slow down before turns.

At highway speed, what can a sudden steering move flip?

  1. The tractor
  2. The last trailer ✓ Correct answer
  3. The front trailer
  4. Nothing at all

Why: A sudden move at highway speed can flip the last trailer even though the tractor stays upright.
