Sample questions

What are the three braking systems combined in an air brake system?

  1. Service, parking, emergency ✓ Correct answer
  2. Service, parking, hydraulic
  3. Primary, secondary, emergency
  4. Service, spring, modulating

Why: An air brake system combines three systems: the service brakes, the parking brakes, and the emergency brakes.

How many separate air systems does a dual air brake system have?

  1. One
  2. Two ✓ Correct answer
  3. Three
  4. Four

Why: A dual air brake system has two separate air systems, a primary and a secondary, with one set of controls.

Before driving a dual air brake system, let pressure build in both systems to what point?

  1. Governor cut-in
  2. Low air warning
  3. Governor cut-out ✓ Correct answer
  4. Safety valve opens

Why: Before driving a dual air brake system, let pressure build to governor cut-out in both systems.

At about what pressure does the air compressor governor cut out and stop pumping air?

  1. 60 psi
  2. 100 psi
  3. 150 psi
  4. 125 psi ✓ Correct answer

Why: The air compressor governor cut-out stops the compressor from pumping air at about 125 psi.

At about what air pressure does the governor cut-in and resume pumping air?

  1. 100 psi ✓ Correct answer
  2. 125 psi
  3. 60 psi
  4. 150 psi

Why: The governor cut-in resumes the compressor pumping air at about 100 psi.

At about what pressure does the safety relief valve open?

  1. 100 psi
  2. 150 psi ✓ Correct answer
  3. 125 psi
  4. 60 psi

Why: The safety relief valve in the supply (wet) tank is set to open at about 150 psi.
