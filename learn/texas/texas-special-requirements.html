Sample questions

I'm hauling a load of crated machinery on a flatbed in Texas, and I'm checking my dimensions before I leave the yard so I don't need an oversize permit. Without a special permit, what is the WIDEST my vehicle plus its load can legally be?

  1. 102 inches, including the load, before a permit is required ✓ Correct answer
  2. 96 inches, including the load, before a permit is required
  3. 108 inches, including the load, before a permit is required
  4. 120 inches, including the load, before a permit is required

Why: A commercial vehicle, including its load, may be no more than 102 inches (8 ft 6 in) wide without a special permit.

I'm hauling a tall reefer trailer across Texas and the dispatcher reminds me I need to clear every overpass on the route without a special oversize permit. As I plan my trip, what is the maximum total height of my vehicle and load that I'm legally allowed to run?

  1. 13 feet 6 inches
  2. 14 feet ✓ Correct answer
  3. 13 feet
  4. 15 feet

Why: The maximum legal height of a vehicle and its load without a special permit is 14 feet, and the driver must clear every bridge and overpass on the route.

I'm loading my tractor-trailer at the dock and the shipper asks how much more freight I can legally take. My truck and trailer already sit at 62,000 lb on the scale, and I have no overweight permit. Without a permit, what is the most my fully loaded rig (vehicle plus load) is allowed to gross on Texas highways?

  1. 76,000 lb
  2. 34,000 lb
  3. 80,000 lb ✓ Correct answer
  4. 90,000 lb

Why: The maximum legal gross weight of a vehicle plus its load without a special overweight permit is 80,000 lb.

I'm driving my CMV down a rural two-lane highway when I come up behind a tractor displaying a reflective orange triangle (the SMV emblem) on its rear. Seeing that emblem, what is the maximum speed I should assume this vehicle was built to travel, so I can plan a safe approach and pass?

  1. 20 mph or less
  2. 45 mph or less
  3. 35 mph or less
  4. 25 mph or less ✓ Correct answer

Why: The orange Slow-Moving Vehicle (SMV) emblem is required on vehicles designed to travel 25 mph or less to warn faster traffic from behind.

I'm doing a pre-trip on a wide box truck and checking that the clearance and marker lamps are the right colors before a night run. Looking at the two clearance lamps mounted up high at the FRONT corners of the truck, which color must they show to be legal?

  1. Amber, because front clearance lamps must show amber to the front ✓ Correct answer
  2. Red, the same color the law requires for the clearance lamps at the rear
  3. White, to match the headlights and stay bright for oncoming traffic
  4. Green, since green indicates the leading edge of a moving vehicle

Why: Clearance and marker lamps that face the front of the vehicle must show amber.

It is just after dusk and I am walking around the back of my oversize-width box truck before pulling out. I need to confirm the clearance lamps mounted high on the REAR of the trailer are the legally required color. Which color must those rear clearance lamps show?

  1. Amber, the same color used for the clearance lamps mounted on the front
  2. Red, matching the rear clearance lamps and reflectors at the back of the vehicle ✓ Correct answer
  3. White, so they throw the brightest light and are seen from the farthest distance
  4. Blue, the color reserved for the highest clearance and marker lamps on a wide load

Why: Clearance lamps and reflectors that face the rear of the vehicle must show red.
