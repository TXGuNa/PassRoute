Sample questions

I already hold a Class B CDL with the school bus (S) endorsement, and tomorrow I start a route picking up and dropping off students. Before I legally drive that loaded school bus, which additional endorsement must I have on my license?

  1. The passenger (P) endorsement, because a school bus carries riders and the P endorsement is required in addition to the S endorsement. ✓ Correct answer
  2. Nothing else, because the S endorsement by itself already covers carrying students on a school bus.
  3. The tank (N) endorsement, because the bus carries a large fuel supply for long daily routes.
  4. A separate motorcycle (M) endorsement, since it proves added skill handling a large vehicle.

Why: Driving a school bus requires the passenger (P) endorsement in addition to the school bus (S) endorsement, since a bus carries riders.

I'm reviewing safety statistics during my school bus training and want to focus my attention where students face the greatest risk of being killed or injured. According to Texas DPS guidance, during which part of my daily route should I be MOST alert to prevent student fatalities?

  1. While crossing railroad tracks, where I must stop 15 to 50 feet before the rails
  2. While loading and unloading students, when they are in the danger zone near the bus ✓ Correct answer
  3. While driving long stretches of open highway, where speeds are highest
  4. While backing the bus in the parking lot, even with a spotter guiding me

Why: Loading and unloading is the most dangerous part of the job, since most school bus fatalities happen when students are in the danger zone near the bus.

I'm stopped to drop off riders and want to keep every student safely within an area I can monitor. Knowing where my school bus's danger zone begins, how far out from the bus should I treat as the high-risk area where a child could be standing in a blind spot?

  1. Only about 1 foot out, since my mirrors cover everything past the bumper line
  2. Roughly 3 feet on each side, just past where the door swings open
  3. About 10 feet out on all sides of the bus, front, rear, and both sides ✓ Correct answer
  4. There is no fixed danger zone as long as my red lights and stop arm are on

Why: The danger zone extends about 10 feet on all sides of the bus—front, rear, and both sides—where children may stand unseen by the driver.

I'm doing my pre-trip and adjusting my school bus mirrors so I can watch the front and side danger zones and keep every student in view, especially the kids crossing about 10 feet in front of the bus. Which set of mirrors is designed to give me that complete coverage?

  1. The two large flat side mirrors alone, since they show the rear and along both sides of the bus
  2. The convex mirrors by themselves, because their wide angle covers the whole area around the bus
  3. A rear-mounted backup camera and the interior overhead mirror, which together watch the loading area
  4. The outside left and right flat and convex mirrors together with the crossover mirrors ✓ Correct answer

Why: The outside left/right flat and convex mirrors must be used together with the crossover mirrors, since no single mirror covers all the front and side danger zones.

As you approach a stop, you first activate the:

  1. Red flashing lights
  2. Amber (yellow) warning lights ✓ Correct answer
  3. The stop arm
  4. The horn

Why: Activate amber lights about 200 ft (or per state law) before the stop, then red at the stop.

You're pulling up to a marked stop to pick up three students. Your amber warning lights have been flashing since well before the stop, and you've now come to a complete halt at the curb. To legally require approaching traffic in both directions to stop while the students load, which action must you take now?

  1. Switch on the red flashing lights and extend the stop arm ✓ Correct answer
  2. Switch off the amber lights and turn on the four-way hazard flashers
  3. Keep only the amber warning lights flashing and open the service door
  4. Sound the horn twice and flash the high-beam headlights at oncoming traffic

Why: Once stopped to load or unload, activate the red flashing lights and extend the stop arm, which together legally command traffic in both directions to stop.
