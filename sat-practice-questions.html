Question 1

If 5x - 3 = 17, what is the value of x?

  1. 3
  2. 4 ✓
  3. 5
  4. 7
Correct answer: B. 4
Why: Add 3 to both sides: 5x = 20. Divide by 5: x = 4.
Question 2

A function is defined by f(x) = 2x + 7. What is the value of f(6)?

  1. 13
  2. 15
  3. 19 ✓
  4. 20
Correct answer: C. 19
Why: Substitute x = 6: f(6) = 2(6) + 7 = 12 + 7 = 19.
Question 3

If f(x) = 3x^2 - 5, what is the value of f(2)?

  1. 1
  2. 7 ✓
  3. 13
  4. 31
Correct answer: B. 7
Why: Substitute x = 2: f(2) = 3(2)^2 - 5 = 3(4) - 5 = 12 - 5 = 7. The correct answer is 7.
Question 4

What is the product of (x + 4)(x - 4)?

  1. x^2 - 16 ✓
  2. x^2 + 16
  3. x^2 - 8x - 16
  4. x^2 - 8
Correct answer: A. x^2 - 16
Why: This is a difference of squares: (x + 4)(x - 4) = x^2 - 4^2 = x^2 - 16. The correct answer is x^2 - 16.
Question 5

In a class, the ratio of boys to girls is 3 to 5. If there are 24 boys, how many girls are in the class?

  1. 8
  2. 14
  3. 40 ✓
  4. 64
Correct answer: C. 40
Why: The ratio boys:girls is 3:5. With 24 boys, each part equals 24/3 = 8. Girls = 5 parts = 5 x 8 = 40.
Question 6

A car travels at a constant speed of 180 miles in 3 hours. At this rate, how many miles will it travel in 5 hours?

  1. 240
  2. 300 ✓
  3. 360
  4. 540
Correct answer: B. 300
Why: The rate is 180/3 = 60 miles per hour. In 5 hours it travels 60 x 5 = 300 miles.
Question 7

A rectangle has a length of 12 centimeters and a width of 4 centimeters. What is the perimeter of the rectangle, in centimeters?

  1. 16
  2. 32 ✓
  3. 48
  4. 64
Correct answer: B. 32
Why: Perimeter = 2(length + width) = 2(12 + 4) = 2(16) = 32 cm. (The distractor 48 is the area; 64 doubles incorrectly.)
Question 8

A rectangular box has dimensions 2 inches by 3 inches by 5 inches. What is the volume of the box, in cubic inches?

  1. 10
  2. 30 ✓
  3. 31
  4. 62
Correct answer: B. 30
Why: Volume = length x width x height = 2 x 3 x 5 = 30 cubic inches. (10 multiplies only two dimensions; 31 is the surface-area-style miscount.)
Question 9

The axolotl is a type of salamander that lives in lakes near Mexico City. Unlike most amphibians, the axolotl does not undergo a full metamorphosis. Instead, it keeps many features of its larval stage, such as feathery external gills, even as an adult. This unusual trait is one reason scientists are so interested in studying the animal. Which choice best states the main idea of the text?

  1. The axolotl is endangered because it lives only near Mexico City.
  2. The axolotl retains larval features into adulthood, making it of interest to scientists. ✓
  3. Most amphibians undergo a full metamorphosis during their lives.
  4. The axolotl's gills are the only feature scientists wish to study.
Correct answer: B. The axolotl retains larval features into adulthood, making it of interest to scientists.
Why: The passage centers on the axolotl keeping larval features as an adult, which interests scientists. The endangerment and gills-only claims overstate details not supported, and the metamorphosis statement is only background contrast, not the main idea.
Question 10

Honeybees communicate the location of food through a behavior called the "waggle dance." A returning forager moves in a figure-eight pattern, and the angle and duration of its central run encode the direction and distance of a food source. Other bees follow the dancer closely, then leave the hive to search in the indicated area. According to the text, what does the waggle dance allow honeybees to do?

  1. Build new sections of the honeycomb more quickly.
  2. Share information about where food can be found. ✓
  3. Defend the hive from approaching predators.
  4. Determine which flowers produce the most nectar.
Correct answer: B. Share information about where food can be found.
Why: The text states the dance encodes direction and distance of a food source so other bees can find it. The other choices describe activities the passage never connects to the dance.
Question 11

Marine biologist Dr. Lena Okafor argues that coral reefs are remarkably ______ ecosystems: even after a severe bleaching event, many reefs can regrow and rebuild their populations of fish and invertebrates within a decade if water conditions improve. Which choice completes the text with the most logical and precise word or phrase?

  1. resilient ✓
  2. decorative
  3. ancient
  4. isolated
Correct answer: A. resilient
Why: "Resilient" means able to recover from difficulty, which fits the reefs' ability to regrow after bleaching. "Decorative" and "ancient" describe other qualities not supported by the recovery context, and "isolated" contradicts the idea of bustling populations rebuilding.
Question 12

When the city council first proposed building a network of bike lanes, the plan seemed ______ to many drivers, who worried that removing car lanes would worsen traffic. Within a year, however, commute times had actually dropped as more people chose to cycle. Which choice completes the text with the most logical and precise word or phrase?

  1. impractical ✓
  2. affordable
  3. temporary
  4. popular
Correct answer: A. impractical
Why: Drivers "worried" the plan would worsen traffic, so they viewed it as "impractical" (not workable). "Affordable" and "temporary" address cost and duration, which are not at issue, and "popular" contradicts the drivers' negative reaction.
Question 13

Many city parks were originally designed for quiet activities like walking and picnicking. Today, however, these same parks must accommodate cyclists, skateboarders, and dog walkers as well. ______ planners are now redesigning paths to keep different users safe and separated. Which choice completes the text with the most logical transition?

  1. As a result, ✓
  2. Nevertheless,
  3. For example,
  4. In contrast,
Correct answer: A. As a result,
Why: The increased variety of park users (cause) leads planners to redesign paths (effect), so a cause-and-effect transition like 'As a result' is correct. 'Nevertheless' and 'In contrast' signal opposition, and 'For example' signals an illustration, none of which fit.
Question 14

The honeybee communicates the location of food through a movement called the waggle dance. The angle of the dance indicates direction, and its duration signals distance. ______ a longer waggle tells other bees that the flowers are farther away. Which choice completes the text with the most logical transition?

  1. However,
  2. For instance, ✓
  3. In conclusion,
  4. On the other hand,
Correct answer: B. For instance,
Why: The sentence gives a specific illustration of how duration signals distance, so 'For instance' is correct. 'However' and 'On the other hand' signal contrast, and 'In conclusion' signals a summary, none of which fit an example.
Question 15

The community garden on Maple Street ______ a popular gathering place for residents who want fresh vegetables and a bit of quiet. Which choice completes the text so that it conforms to the conventions of Standard English?

  1. have become
  2. are becoming
  3. has become ✓
  4. were becoming
Correct answer: C. has become
Why: The singular subject "garden" requires a singular verb. "Has become" agrees in number; "have become," "are becoming," and "were becoming" are all plural and do not agree with the singular subject.
Question 16

When Maria finished painting the mural, ______ stepped back to admire the bright colors she had chosen. Which choice completes the text so that it conforms to the conventions of Standard English?

  1. they
  2. it
  3. she ✓
  4. we
Correct answer: C. she
Why: The pronoun must agree with its antecedent, "Maria," a single female individual. "She" agrees in number and gender; "they," "it," and "we" do not match the singular feminine antecedent.
